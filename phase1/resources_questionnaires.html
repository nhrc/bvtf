Middle School Youth

Primary Concerns

  • Storage
  • Fellowship Hall
  • Accessibility
  • Restrooms
  • Exterior (Basketball Hoops)
  • Chapel

Questionnaire

See the original

Considering the facility "as is", what enhances the effectiveness of the RIOT ministry?

  • Youth room is right by an entryway, easy for us to greet kids and parents and help other parents find their way to Kid Connection if necessary
  • Office is close in case we need to make copies
  • There are some bathroom options close (but boys is downstairs and girls have to use the single room with the heavy door unless they go downstairs and around the corner)
  • Building is great for playing Sardines
  • Coffee area is great for our size group once in awhile
  • Narthex has great small group spot for when we want to break apart for discussions§ Plenty of seating for our current group size (approx. 16 kids and 4 leaders)

Considering the facility "as is", what impedes the effectiveness of the RIOT ministry?

  • Bathrooms on different levels and with heavy door can be difficult for some of the kids
  • Chapel Room is inadequate
    • Space constraints for games (have to go outside or hope Fellowship Room isn’t in use)
    • Gets hot in there sometimes (can’t open windows)
    • Love that stained glass is preserved, but not the best in a youth room where kids can get rowdy sometimes
    • Hanging lights are sometimes a hazard… Rather, they have close calls with being damaged

List and rank recommendations

  1. Indoor space for run-around games
  2. WiFi that is reliable and accessible anywhere in the Chapel Room and more places in the church
  3. Sound System in youth room (for listening to music or integrating with laptop/projector for YouTube videos
  4. New TV that's easy to hook up laptop, iPad or DVD player
  5. Move basketball hoops towards playground (away from mid-parking lot where ball rolls towards road
  6. Better kitchenette for snacks
    • Fridge for storing pop (current is too small for 2-liters
    • Sink for cleaning out reusable cups/dishes
    • Slightly bigger counter for snacks or pizza parties
  7. Foursquare area outside

If you could change the layout of the church campus, etc, what would it look like?

  • Basketball hoop away from road
  • Bathrooms and kitchen-type facilities closer or on same level
  • Like youth room near entry/exit (always able to greet parents/kids and keep in touch with parents better), if it were closer to playground area, would give kids safer spot to hang out while waiting for rides rather than near front sign and road.

Currently how many kids are in the RIOT program?

  • 16

Do you expect to see growth in the RIOT program in the next year(s)?

  • Not a ton based on the now - kids sometimes bring friends, but not regularly
  • Probably will stay under 20 kids for next couple of years
  • Biggest concerns are the space and future growth of the RIOT group. Eventually we will outgrow the chapel

Small Groups

Primary Concerns

  • Narthex
  • Fellowship Hall
  • Classrooms
  • Nursery

Questionnaire

See the original

Does the layout of the facility influence the work you are currently doing?

  • Most small groups currently meet at homes
    • Homes provide sense of intimacy and comfort
    • There are few rooms that are appropriate meeting spaces for small groups
    • Aren't enough TVs at church for DVD resources to accomodate all groups
    • Easier to meet at homes for groups with small children
  • Small Group Ministry Team meets in chapel, which works okay but is not ideal

Considering the facility "as is", what impedes the effectiveness of your ministry?

  • Traffic flow by coffee area (impedes creating connections with one another)
  • Fellowship Room could be expanded and updated for larger group gathers held by our team and others
  • Limits in space means limit in our capacity to grow and welcome others
  • There aren't many small group friendly spaces in our church
  • There aren't many team meeting spaces in our church
  • Classrooms design for table/chair feel or are design for small children
  • Rooms and resources not easy to access, especially TV/DVD sets for groups to use

Please list recommendations:

  • Spaces for groups to meet onsite rather than in homes
    • Comfortable chairs than can be arranged in a circle or arranged as needed
    • Clearly marked rooms to be easier to locate groups people want to join
    • Rooms need to be comfortable and provide sense of welcome
    • Rooms need to be private enough for people to want to share freely
    • These rooms could also be used by teams for meeting spaces
    • Could be fun to have a different theme for each room
  • TVs and other resources that would be easily available

What do you believe the congregation likes about our facility?

  • Ease of access to main office
  • Newer areas are attractive
  • Pretty building
  • "Feel" of sanctuary - warm & inviting
  • Opportunities to fellowship right behind sanctuary

Do you believe the existing facility attracts visitors?

  • Not aesthetically pleasing from road with pavement corner
  • Confusing entrance
  • Poor condition of lot
  • Prior landscaping makes look old
  • Interior redecorated areas (narthex) are welcoming, but rest of decorations/building needs updating
  • Sanctuary light fixtures are dated
  • 10 year old quilts in sanctuary
  • Impression is that some areas are neglected

Do you believe our facility influences whether or not a visitor returns to church?

  • Yes - the space/tightness must be full enough so visitors don't stick out, but still feel like there is room
  • Church numbers only grow based on having space to grow
  • Confusion over where to go doesn't make one feel welcomed
  • Parking lot and pot holes not welcoming

  • Sanctuary has an outdated image
  • Consistory room is too small, worn out, out of style

  • Classrooms are too spread out and not flexible for varying class sizes
  • No adult classrooms

  • Steps up to stage are difficult for weddings
